Mathura Holi: Rangbhari Ekadashi & Lathmar Holi 2025

Celebrate Holi in Mathura – Vrindavan – Barsana – Gokul – Nandgaon, the Land of Lord Krishna!

India’s Holi is celebrated with a spirit of joy and happiness, signifying the start of the Spring season and Lord Krishna’s deep love for the festival.

Mathura is one of India’s holiest cities, and the ideal place to experience this magical festival. Visitors join locals in celebrations that last several days, featuring devotional rituals, dancing, singing, and showers of multi-colored powder.

The atmosphere is full of joy and excitement—Holi in Mathura is truly a breathtaking experience. The Holi Festival lasts an entire month, filling the region with vibrant colors and happiness.

From Gokul to Vrindavan, Barsana to Nandgaon, the celebrations attract visitors from across India and the world. TV channels, influencers, and photographers often visit to capture the beauty of Mathura Holi.

Signature events like Barsana Lathmar Holi, Falen Ki Holi, and Dauji Huranga bring unique traditions to life—with blessings, fire rituals, and joyful play. Each celebration is a magical glimpse into the devotional culture of Braj.
